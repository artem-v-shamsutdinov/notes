Nuclear fusion energy defines the midpoint of the progression

MTABS time progression was based on the idea of an hourglass progression in the development of humanity. It is now clear that the neck of the hourglass maps to the first utilization of nuclear fusion for energy (possibly the first engineering net energy gain). Nuclear fusion (of one form or another) will likely be the main way we get energy for millions of years to come. Based on MTABS logic usage of nuclear fusion by humanity will naturally progress through at least stages:

  • Utilizing Earth-based deuterium
  • Utilizing Solar System-based fusion material
  • Utilizing hydrogen from the Sun directly

After that, the logic that followed MTABS predicts us moving to more advanced sources of energy, of which we are not yet sure (could be for exampleÂ anti-matter, dark-energy, harnessing black holes, or just plain reducing the sizes of stars at risk of going novae or supernovae).

The order of magnitude increase was introduced to signify progressively longer periods of time in which we'll find ourselves in (or have found ourselves in the past). The constant of 7 was picked in order to find the best pace possible at which we could progress forward.

Three planets as the measure

The key deciding factor was the timeframe for introducing Solar System-external, dry surface-based civilizations to our (at least seemly) vacant lots of Venus and Mars. We would have to be ready in time and at the same time, given the constant presence of cosmic calamities around us, we wouldn't take too much time before it would be too late to help civilizations on the brink of extinction. The assumption was made that the lower bound for this period was 300 years and the upper bound was 1,000 years. Thus 700 years from now was picked as the "middle of the road" timeframe that would be acceptable to both (expecting only one arrival at a time, with the second coming later) parties.

Inspiration and logic

The timeframe was specified largely on the inspiration of an incoming dream and subsequent ideas and the need to help others survive. However, it also makes sense on a purely logical level - as soon as a civilization with nearby vacant lots is capable of existing with other civilization(s) in the same star system it will be asked to do so, simply because there is always a civilization in need due to constantly on-going stellar events that threaten its existence.

The aquatics

The expected existence of (possibly technologically) intelligent species within the Solar System factored in as something that needs to be addressed before the introduction of "other children from burnt-out houses". Thus the timeframe of 70-700 years from now was introduced, with the necessary preparations for it coming in at 0-70 years.

Logically, introducing aquatic (and thus non-territorially conflicting) species to Earth would prepare us for co-habitation of the Solar System "house". The external (to our Solar System) civilizations would be dry surface-based but would be safely separated from us by being on other planets. The aquatics to be introduced to our own oceans would co-habit the planet ("room") with us. However, they are not to inhabit the continents, thus preventing the predicted fight to extermination between dry surface-based intelligent species.

MTABS argues that there are (near and, as added in the notes, at least in the case of Pluto fully) technologically intelligent aquatics. From that, the logic follows that the morally right thing to do is to "show them God's Light" and give them a chance to eventually develop space-faring civilizations. The argument also assumes that the gains would be mutual and that the Earth would gain custodians of the oceans that would protect them from overuse and mismanagement. On top of that, working with the less technologically advanced aquatics before the arrival of more technologically advanced "housemates" would ease us into the coming conditions.

MTABS logic assumes that we have several (potentially) technologically intelligent aquatic species in oceans of various moons/dwarf planets. This implies that some of them will be more advanced and thus more territorially aggressive (or just plain old more aggressive). The logical assumption then is to first populate less advanced aquatics and more peaceful aquatics to ensure that they will have a place in the (what is assumed to necessarily be) balance of forces in the extraterrestrially populated parts of the oceans.

The progression Ages

As is apparent now, the general progression is split up into 4 Ages:

  • Earth - 2 phases
  • Solar System - 3 phases
  • Sun - 2 phases
  • Galaxy - 3 phases

The first age consists of Phase 1 (preparation for aquatic species) and Phase 2 (population of the first aquatic species) and lasts up to roughly 700 years.

The second age consists of Phase 3 (population of additional aquatics, initial population of Venus and Mars), Phase 4 (taking care of the Solar System, in a collective fashion), and Phase 5 (utilizing the resources of the Solar System to grow our combined industrial capacities and technological abilities to take on the tasks in the next age).

The third age consists of Phase 6 (initial management of Sun's surface) and Phase 7 (extracting heavier elements from the Sun's core, finally achieving its eternity).
